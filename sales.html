Know Your Personas

Six key stakeholders you'll encounter in B2B search conversations

Buyer Personas

The Rushed Buyer
"I know what I need, get me there NOW"

What they care about: Speed, accuracy, minimal clicks

Lead with:

  • Auto Complete
  • Part Number Normalization
  • Recent Search
  • Recently Ordered
  • Voice Search

Opening question:

"How quickly can your repeat buyers reorder?"
The Exploring Buyer
"Show me my options, I'm comparing"

What they care about: Options, alternatives, inspiration

Lead with:

  • Faceted Search
  • Visual Filters
  • Recommendations
  • Related Searches
  • Trending Search

Opening question:

"How do buyers discover products they didn't know you carried?"
The Technical Buyer
"I need the exact spec, the right part"

What they care about: Precision, specs, alternatives, compliance

Lead with:

  • Semantic Search
  • Cross-Brand Equivalents
  • Part Number Normalization
  • Custom Products (Kitting)
  • Searchable Certifications

Opening question:

"How do you handle part number variations and cross-references?"

Internal Stakeholders

The Merchandiser
"I need to shape what buyers see"

What they care about: Control, visibility, content quality

Lead with:

  • Curations
  • Search Dashboard
  • Query Behaviour Labeller
  • Search Sandbox
  • AI Enrichment Dashboard

Opening question:

"Who controls what buyers see in search results? How do you test changes?"
The Business Leader
"I need to see the ROI"

What they care about: Revenue, conversion, ROI

Lead with:

  • Analytics
  • Search Dashboard
  • Recommendations (basket size)

Opening question:

"How do you measure search's impact on revenue?"
The IT/Ops Leader
"I need it stable, secure, manageable"

What they care about: Stability, governance, testing

Lead with:

  • Configuration Management
  • Search Sandbox
  • A/B Testing
  • AI Enrichment Dashboard

Opening question:

"How do you test and deploy search changes safely? Who approves AI content?"

Discovery Questions

Questions to uncover needs and qualify opportunities

Findability

  • • "How long does it take your average buyer to find what they need?"
  • • "What's your site search usage rate?"
  • • "How do buyers typically search — keywords, part numbers, descriptions?"
  • • "How large is your catalog? How many SKUs?"

Understanding

  • • "What percentage of searches use part numbers vs. descriptions?"
  • • "Do buyers use industry jargon your catalog doesn't?"
  • • "What happens when someone searches for a brand name?"
  • • "How do you handle abbreviations and misspellings?"

Relevance

  • • "Do your merchandisers feel they can influence what appears first?"
  • • "How do you promote high-margin or strategic products?"
  • • "Are the right products appearing at the top of results?"
  • • "How do you handle seasonal or promotional content?"

Cross-sell

  • • "What's your current attach rate or basket size?"
  • • "How do you suggest related products today?"
  • • "Do you show accessories, consumables, or frequently bought together?"
  • • "What happens when the primary brand is out of stock?"

Reordering

  • • "What percentage of your orders are repeat purchases?"
  • • "How do repeat buyers currently reorder?"
  • • "Do you surface purchase history in search?"
  • • "How many clicks to reorder a previous item?"

Zero Results

  • • "What's your zero-result rate?"
  • • "What do buyers see when search returns nothing?"
  • • "Where do those buyers go after hitting a dead end?"
  • • "Do you track abandonment from zero results?"

Control

  • • "Who makes changes to search today? IT or business?"
  • • "How long does a search change take to implement?"
  • • "Can merchandisers test changes before going live?"
  • • "How do you handle campaigns and promotions in search?"

Visibility

  • • "How do you measure search performance today?"
  • • "Do you know your top searches? Your failed searches?"
  • • "Can you see search-to-conversion rates?"
  • • "How do you identify optimization opportunities?"

Qualifying Questions

Data Readiness

  • • "How detailed are your product descriptions?"
  • • "Do you have structured attributes for most products?"
  • • "Do you have search analytics or click data?"
  • • "Can you connect user accounts to search behavior?"

Capacity & Timeline

  • • "Who will manage search day-to-day?"
  • • "What's your timeline for this project?"
  • • "What's your budget range?"
  • • "Are there hard launch dates we need to hit?"

Example Conversations

Scenario: "Our search is broken"

Client: "Our search is broken. Buyers complain they can't find things."

You: "That's a common pain point. Let me understand better — when buyers search, are they using part numbers, descriptions, or brand names mostly?"

Client: "Mix of everything. Part numbers, product names, sometimes just categories."

You: "And when they search for a part number with spaces or dashes, does it still find the right product?"

Client: "Actually, no. That's a big complaint."

You: "That's a common issue — Part Number Normalization solves it. 'ABC-123' and 'ABC123' would both match. What's your zero-result rate? Do you track when searches return nothing?"

Features surfaced: Part Number Normalization, Auto Complete, Zero Results Recovery

Scenario: "We want to increase basket size"

Client: "We want to increase average order value. Cross-selling is weak."

You: "What's your current attach rate for accessories and consumables?"

Client: "Low. Buyers find what they searched for and check out."

You: "Do you show 'frequently bought together' or 'you may also need' anywhere?"

Client: "No, we don't have that capability."

You: "A Recommendation Engine learns from purchase patterns and surfaces relevant add-ons. What about when a brand is out of stock — do you suggest alternatives?"

Client: "No, they just see out of stock."

You: "That's a missed opportunity. Cross-Brand Equivalents can say 'This brand isn't available, but here's a compatible alternative.' Do you have cross-reference data?"

Features surfaced: Recommendation Engine, Cross-Brand Equivalents, Related Searches

Scenario: "Our merchandisers are frustrated"

Client: "Our merchandising team can't do anything with search. It's all IT."

You: "How long does it take to make a search change today?"

Client: "Weeks. We have to file a ticket and wait."

You: "So if you want to promote a seasonal item or run a campaign, you can't do it quickly?"

Client: "Exactly. By the time it's live, the promotion is over."

You: "Curations give merchandisers direct control — pin products, boost results, create promotional placements. All through a dashboard, no code. With a Search Sandbox, they can preview changes before going live."

Client: "That would be a game-changer."

Features surfaced: Curations, Search Sandbox, Configuration Management, Search Dashboard

Common Objections

"Our data isn't ready"

Response: "That's where AI Enrichment helps. We can generate missing descriptions and attributes, with human review before publishing. Let's assess what you have and identify the fastest path forward."

"We don't have budget for everything"

Response: "We don't have to do everything at once. We can start with high-impact, low-complexity features and add more over time. What are your must-haves for launch?"

"We need this live in 6 weeks"

Response: "Tight timelines mean we focus on MVP for some features and polish later. Let's identify what must be perfect at launch vs. what can iterate."

"We don't have anyone to manage search"

Response: "Two options: we invest more in self-service features with strong guardrails, or we include ongoing managed support. Which fits better with your model?"
